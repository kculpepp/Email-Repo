Brain injury research validates new assessment tool.

New research has revealed four distinct patterns of symptoms that occur after traumatic brain injury in military service members. This is crucial because TBI is a major concern for the military, with more than 294,000 individuals affected between 2000 and 2013.

According to experts, the research also validates a new questionnaire that is used to assess an individual's health-related quality of life after TBI. This tool evaluates 20 subscales in the areas of physical and emotional health, cognition and social participation.
