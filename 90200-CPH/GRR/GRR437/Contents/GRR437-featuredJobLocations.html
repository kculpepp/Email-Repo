Woodland Hills, CA

Nestled against the Santa Monica Mountains in the San Fernando Valley, this city is known for its outdoor activities and pleasant Mediterranean climate. Along the western boundary of Woodland Hills is the large Upper Las Virgenes Canyon Open Space Preserve, a gorgeous park with a network of trails for hiking, mountain biking, and equestrian rides.

Baltimore, MD

The largest city in Maryland, Baltimore sits along the Patapsco River, an arm of the Chesapeake Bay. It played a key role in events of the American Revolution and boasts 72 designated historic districts. These include Inner Harbor, famous for its shops and museums; Fells Point, which was featured in Sleepless in Seattle; and Little Italy, where you’ll find superb Italian food.

Fallon, NV

Fallon is known as the “Oasis of Nevada.” In town, you’ll find excellent art galleries, museums, and nightclubs. Fallon is surrounded by wide-open spaces, internationally known wetlands, Pony Express trails, state parks, hiking opportunities, and places to go off-road. It also offers historical adventures in places like Grimes Point and Hidden Cave.
