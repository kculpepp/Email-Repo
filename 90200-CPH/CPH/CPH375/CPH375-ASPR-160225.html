Recognizing red flags in a physician's CV.

A CV is often the first introduction you have to an applicant. Therefore, it's a good place to spot red flags early on. Each section of a physician's CV provides important information, and what is not included is often as, or more, important than what is. The goal of clearing away red flags is to make sure the physician is who he or she claims to be and is properly qualified to care for patients at your facility. Red flags don't necessarily eliminate a physician from consideration, but they do mean extra care should be given to getting satisfactory answers to questions in red flag areas.

For tips on spotting red flags, see our blog post "What to Look for When Screening a Physician."
