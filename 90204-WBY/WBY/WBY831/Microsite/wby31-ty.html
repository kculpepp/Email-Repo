Together We Can Put Out the Flames of Physician Burnout

The Problem

Burnout is a physical or mental collapse caused by overwork or stress. In particular, physicians suffer from burnout more often than any other profession.

The Cause

Personal life-related stress, healthcare reform, poor job fit, and the overall state of the U.S. economy are a few of the reported causes of burnout.

Identifying Burnout

Burnout comes in many forms, but generally, a deficient work/life balance is a sure sign of its effects.

The Solution

There are options for physicians who are burned-out. Teaching, advising, or working locum tenens are excellent ways to combat the effects of burnout.
